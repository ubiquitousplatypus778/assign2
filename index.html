Identifying Problems

In this project, I analyzed and redesigned the homepage of Mirabelles Bakery, which is a bakery located in South Burlington, Vermont. I found the design of their website to be a rather poor reflection of the quality of their pastries and decided to remodel its homepage.

WebAIM WAVE identifies the following major problems:

1. Low contrast between background color and text in navigation bar and footer.

2. A redundant link: “Today’s Specials” in the nav bar leads back to the page itself.

I don’t think the low contrast is very concerning compared to other flaws in the webpage. However, the redundancy of links and buttons should be removed.

I also identified the following problems in terms of usability, memorability, and learnability:

Usability

The long lists of menu items make it hard for users to locate items. Also, the food items are not listed in any particular order. The layout of buttons and categories is also quite random, which hinders usability.

Memorability

The webpage is not memorable as a homepage: there are no pictures, only large blocks of text. It doesn’t even show the name of the bakery.

Learnability

The repetition of items such as “Cake” and "Breakfast" in both the navigation bar and the text creates confusion. Also, there are two buttons: “ORDER CAKES ONLINE” and “ONLINE STORE” that lead to the same page, which decreases learnability.

Low-fidelity Wireframing

As a first step, I created the following 3 low-fidelity wireframes, one for each of the screen sizes: desktop, tablet, and mobile. Since the original webpage is the homepage of the bakery, I decided to remove the long menus and refurnish it with more eye-catching images. The menus can be made accessible through links beneath the images and will not be a major component of the homepage.
